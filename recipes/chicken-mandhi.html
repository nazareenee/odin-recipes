Chicken Mandhi Recipe

Home

Description

Chicken Mandhi is a traditional Arabian dish that has become very popular in Kerala and across South India. It consists of fragrant rice cooked with mild spices, served with tender, slow-cooked chicken. What makes mandhi unique is its subtle flavor—unlike biryani, it is less spicy and focuses more on the natural taste of the meat, often infused with smoky aroma. The dish is usually accompanied by sauces like tomato chutney and mayonnaise, adding extra flavor to each bite.

The cooking method of mandhi is also special, traditionally prepared in an underground pit called a “tandoor-style” oven, where the chicken is roasted and the rice absorbs its juices. This results in juicy, flavorful meat and soft, aromatic rice. Today, mandhi is widely enjoyed in restaurants and has become a favorite for gatherings due to its rich taste and appealing presentation.

Ingredients

  • 1 whole chicken (cleaned, cut or whole)
  • 2 cups basmati rice
  • 2 onions (sliced)
  • 2 tomatoes (chopped)
  • 2 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 3–4 tbsp oil or butter
  • Whole spices (cardamom, cloves, cinnamon, bay leaf)
  • Mandhi spice mix (or mix of turmeric, chili powder, cumin, coriander)
  • Salt (to taste)
  • 4 cups chicken stock or water
  • Saffron soaked in milk (optional)

Steps

  1. Wash and soak rice for 20–30 minutes.
  2. Marinate chicken with spices, salt, and ginger-garlic paste for at least 1 hour.
  3. Heat oil/butter in a pot and sauté onions until golden.
  4. Add tomatoes and cook until soft.
  5. Add whole spices and mix well.
